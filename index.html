India Basics: Rights & Responsibilities

  • Feeding community animals kindly and cleanly is widely recognised; be respectful of neighbours and keep the area tidy.
  • Relocating community dogs is discouraged; stable, sterilised groups reduce conflicts.
  • If issues arise: talk to your Resident Welfare Association (RWA) or local municipal veterinary officer; propose fixed feeding spots and ABC drives.
  • In cruelty situations, you can seek help from local NGOs or police. Maintain calm, factual records.

Note: Regulations can vary by city/municipality. For exact legal steps, contact your local animal welfare board/NGO or municipal veterinary department.
